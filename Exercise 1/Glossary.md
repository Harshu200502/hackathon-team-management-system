## Glossary

This glossary defines key terms, acronyms, and concepts used in the **Hackathon Team Management System** project to ensure a common understanding among all stakeholders.

---

### A

**Admin**  
A system user with administrative privileges who can monitor and manage teams and system activities.

**Actor**  
An external entity (user or system) that interacts with the system, such as Student, Team Leader, or Admin.

---

### C

**CASE Tool**  
Computer-Aided Software Engineering tool (e.g., StarUML, Visual Paradigm) used for creating UML diagrams and models.

**Class Diagram**  
A UML diagram that represents system classes, their attributes, methods, and relationships.

---

### F

**Functional Requirement (FR)**  
A specification of what the system should do, describing system behavior and functionalities.

---

### G

**Glossary**  
A list of important project terms and their definitions.

---

### H

**Hackathon**  
A time-bound collaborative event where participants form teams to develop technical solutions or software projects.

---

### I

**Invitation**  
A request sent by a Team Leader to a Student to join a specific team.

---

### N

**Non-Functional Requirement (NFR)**  
A specification that defines system quality attributes such as performance, security, usability, and reliability.

---

### R

**Role**  
A responsibility assigned to a team member within a team (e.g., Developer, Designer, Tester).

---

### S

**Sequence Diagram**  
A UML diagram that shows object interactions arranged in a time sequence.

**Stakeholder**  
Any individual or group that has an interest in or influence over the project.

**Student**  
A registered user who participates in hackathons and joins teams.

---

### T

**Team**  
A group of students collaborating in a hackathon.

**Team Leader**  
A student responsible for creating and managing a team.

---

### U

**Use Case**  
A description of how an actor interacts with the system to achieve a specific goal.

**Use Case Diagram**  
A UML diagram showing relationships between actors and use cases.
